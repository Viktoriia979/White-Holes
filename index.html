White Holes:

White holes are theoretical objects in space that are considered the opposite of black holes. While a black hole pulls matter and light inward, a white hole would push matter and light outward and would not allow anything to enter. White holes are predicted by some mathematical solutions to Einstein's theory of general relativity, but no white hole has ever been observed.

Scientists do not know whether white holes can actually form in nature. Some theories suggest that a white hole could be connected to a black hole through a wormhole, while others propose that white holes might have existed briefly during the early stages of the universe. However, these ideas remain highly speculative and have not been confirmed by observations.

If you approached a white hole, you would be unable to enter it because its gravity and energy would force matter away. Objects and light would be expelled outward from the white hole, making it appear very bright. Since white holes are only theoretical, scientists are still studying whether they could exist and what their properties might be.
